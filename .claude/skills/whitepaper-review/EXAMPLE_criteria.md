## Evaluation Criteria Provided by User

**Target audience:** 
DARPA program managers with technical backgrounds in autonomy and robotics, but not necessarily deep learning experts. Decision-makers will include both technical reviewers and budget/program office staff.

**Primary goal:** 
Win Phase II SBIR funding ($1.5M over 24 months) to advance our continuous-time transformer for real-time object tracking. This follows our successful Phase I demonstration.

**Specific judging criteria:**

Based on the DARPA BAA HR001234-S-5678, proposals are evaluated on:

1. **Technical Merit (40%)**
   - Scientific and technical approach is sound, feasible, and innovative
   - Methodology is rigorous and well-justified
   - Technical risks are identified with mitigation strategies
   - Approach shows clear advancement beyond Phase I results

2. **Team Qualifications (30%)**
   - Key personnel have relevant expertise and track record
   - Past performance demonstrates ability to deliver
   - Team structure is appropriate for the proposed effort
   - Access to necessary facilities and resources

3. **Impact and Relevance (20%)**
   - Potential contribution to DoD mission needs
   - Clear path to military applications and transition
   - Quantified performance improvements over current methods
   - Addresses gaps in existing capabilities

4. **Cost Realism (10%)**
   - Budget is reasonable and well-justified
   - Resource allocation aligns with technical approach
   - Labor hours and rates are appropriate
   - No unexplained costs or gaps

**Additional context:**

- Phase I reviewers praised our technical approach but noted we were "too academic" in presentation
- Competing teams include traditional Kalman filter approaches (established but limited) and other deep learning methods (less proven)
- Our key differentiator is continuous-time attention with uncertainty quantification
- We need to emphasize deployment readiness, robustness to noisy inputs, and real-time performance
- Previous feedback: "Strong on theory, needs more concrete transition strategy and military use case specifics"
- Page limit: 25 pages including figures (currently at 28, need to be concise)
- Submission deadline: 3 weeks from now
- Must demonstrate we can handle classified data environments (we have facility clearance but should mention this)

**Tone preferences:**
- Professional but accessible — avoid heavy math notation in main text (move to appendices)
- Confident without being arrogant
- Emphasize practical deployment over theoretical elegance
- Use concrete examples and metrics rather than vague claims
