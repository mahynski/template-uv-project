## Quick Reference: Common Evaluation Frameworks

### DARPA BAA Evaluation Factors
- Technical merit and scientific/technical approach
- Potential contribution and relevance to DARPA mission
- Offeror's capabilities and related experience
- Cost realism and reasonableness

### VC Pitch Deck Rubric
- Problem: Clear, significant, urgent
- Market: Large, growing, accessible
- Solution: Innovative, defensible, scalable
- Traction: Evidence of product-market fit
- Team: Domain expertise, execution capability
- Business model: Path to profitability
- Competitive landscape: Sustainable advantage
- Financial projections: Realistic and compelling
- Ask: Clear funding amount and use of funds

### NIH Grant Review Criteria
- Significance: Importance of problem and potential impact
- Investigators: Qualifications and track record
- Innovation: Novel concepts, approaches, or methodologies
- Approach: Strategy, methods, and feasibility
- Environment: Institutional support and resources

### Academic Journal Standards
- Novelty and originality
- Technical soundness and rigor
- Reproducibility
- Clarity of presentation
- Significance of results
- Comparison to related work
- Limitations and future work

### Government Contract Evaluation (FAR)
- Technical approach and understanding of requirements
- Past performance and relevant experience
- Key personnel qualifications
- Management approach
- Price reasonableness and realism

### Internal Business Case Review
- Strategic alignment with company goals
- Market opportunity and competitive position
- Financial projections (ROI, payback period)
- Resource requirements (people, budget, time)
- Risk assessment and mitigation
- Success metrics and milestones
- Alternative approaches considered

## Tips for Filling This Out

1. **Be specific:** "Get funding" → "Win $500K Phase II SBIR award from DARPA"
2. **Include weights:** If criteria are weighted, specify the percentages
3. **Quote actual rubrics:** If you have official evaluation criteria, copy them exactly
4. **Name your concerns:** What keeps you up at night about this document?
5. **Identify constraints:** Page limits, format requirements, required sections
6. **Note the competition:** Who/what are you being compared against?
7. **Set tone expectations:** Formal? Conversational? Aggressive? Conservative?

The more detail you provide, the more actionable and relevant the feedback will be.
